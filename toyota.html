Toyota's first car was the Toyota Model AA, introduced in 1936. The Model AA was a passenger car that resembled the Chevrolet Master, a popular car of the time. It marked Toyota's entry into the automobile industry, following its establishment as a division of the Toyoda Automatic Loom Works in 1933, which was originally focused on manufacturing looms. The Model AA was powered by a 3.4-liter, six-cylinder engine and was designed to be a reliable, affordable car for the Japanese market.

Toyota has expanded its lineup to include fully electric models like the bZ4X and continues to invest heavily in hydrogen fuel cell technology with the Mirai.
