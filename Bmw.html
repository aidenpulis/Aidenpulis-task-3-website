BMW

Its roots trace back to the merger of three companies: Rapp Motorenwerke, Bayerische Flugzeugwerke, and Fahrzeugfabrik Eisenach. After World War I, BMW shifted focus from aircraft engines to motorcycles and automobiles due to restrictions on German aviation. The brand's first motorcycle, the R32, launched in 1923, and its first car, the BMW 3/15, came in 1929 after acquiring the Dixi brand.

BMW (Bayerische Motoren Werke) initially started as an aircraft engine manufacturer in 1916, but its first car arrived a bit later. Over the decades, BMW has evolved into a leading manufacturer of premium vehicles, known for combining performance, innovation, and design. The company has introduced various models across different segments, including the 3 Series, 5 Series, and 7 Series sedans, as well as the X Series SUVs. BMW's commitment to innovation is evident in its development of electric and hybrid vehicles, such as the BMW i3 and i8, and more recently, the i4 and iX models.
